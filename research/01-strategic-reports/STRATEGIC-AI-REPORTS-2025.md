# IBM Strategic AI Reports 2025

## Executive Summary

IBM's 2025 strategic AI reports reveal a decisive shift toward **agentic AI** as the dominant enterprise theme, with 46% of executives actively scaling AI initiatives. The research, based on surveys of thousands of global leaders, shows AI investments expected to double within two years, with 85% of organizations anticipating positive ROI from scaled AI by 2027.

## Key Reports & Findings

### 1. Business Trends 2025 Report

**Research Scope:**
- 400 global leaders surveyed across 17 industries
- Focus on enterprise AI adoption and scaling strategies
- Publication: 2025

**Key Findings:**
- **46% of executives scaling AI in 2025** - significant year-over-year increase
- **Agentic AI emerges as the dominant strategic theme**
- Organizations moving beyond pilot programs to production-scale deployments
- Cross-industry consensus on AI as competitive necessity

**Strategic Implications:**
- First-mover advantage diminishing as AI becomes table stakes
- Need for enterprise-wide AI transformation strategies
- Infrastructure and governance frameworks becoming critical differentiators

**Link:** https://www.ibm.com/thought-leadership/institute-business-value/en-us/report/business-trends-2025

### 2. CEO Study on AI (May 2025)

**Research Scope:**
- 2,000 CEOs surveyed globally
- Executive-level AI strategy and investment analysis
- Focus on leadership perspectives and decision-making

**Key Findings:**

**Investment & Adoption:**
- **AI investments expected to more than double in next 2 years**
- **61% actively adopting AI agents today** (not just piloting)
- Shift from experimentation to operational deployment
- C-suite commitment to AI transformation solidified

**Business Value Realization:**
- **52% realizing value from generative AI beyond cost reduction**
  - Revenue generation applications gaining traction
  - Product innovation and market expansion use cases
  - Customer experience enhancement driving measurable value

**ROI Expectations:**
- **85% expect positive ROI from scaled AI by 2027**
- 3-year investment horizon becoming standard
- Focus on measurable business outcomes, not just efficiency

**Strategic Priorities:**
1. Scaling from pilots to production
2. Building AI-native operating models
3. Developing AI governance frameworks
4. Investing in workforce transformation

**CEO Concerns:**
- Talent acquisition and retention for AI roles
- Data quality and availability challenges
- Ethical AI and regulatory compliance
- Integration with legacy systems

### 4. Chief Data Officers Study (November 2025)

**Research Scope:**
- 1,700 CDOs surveyed worldwide
- Focus on data strategy for AI scaling
- Evolution of CDO role and responsibilities

**Key Findings:**

**AI Readiness Gap:**
- **Widening gap between AI ambition and readiness**

- Organizational silos hindering data access

**Data Strategy Evolution:**

- Real-time data processing becoming critical

**Organizational Impact:**
- CDO role expanding to include AI governance
- Collaboration with Chief AI Officers increasing
- Data teams growing in size and strategic importance

## Cross-Report Insights & Themes

### Agentic AI as Strategic Priority
All 2025 reports identify agentic AI as the defining characteristic of the next wave of enterprise AI adoption. Organizations are moving beyond chatbots and assistants to autonomous agents that can:
- Execute multi-step workflows independently
- Make decisions within defined parameters
- Coordinate with other agents and systems
- Learn and adapt from outcomes

### Investment Acceleration
- **Current State:** AI budgets doubling year-over-year
- **2027 Outlook:** 85% expect positive ROI from scaled AI
- **Focus Areas:** Infrastructure, talent, data platforms, governance

### Value Realization Beyond Cost Reduction
CEOs report 52% are seeing value beyond efficiency gains:
- **Revenue Generation:** New AI-enabled products and services
- **Market Expansion:** AI-powered personalization enabling new customer segments
- **Innovation Velocity:** Faster product development and time-to-market
- **Competitive Advantage:** AI as differentiator, not just utility

### Governance & Ethics Rise in Priority
- Organizations investing in AI ethics see 30% higher operating profit
- Regulatory compliance becoming competitive advantage
- Explainability and transparency critical for enterprise adoption
- Board-level oversight of AI initiatives becoming standard

## Strategic Recommendations for Enterprises

### For CEOs:
1. **Commit to enterprise-wide AI transformation** - pilots alone won't deliver competitive advantage
2. **Invest in data foundation** - address data quality and accessibility gaps now
3. **Build AI governance early** - don't wait for regulatory mandate
4. **Focus on talent ecosystem** - mix of hiring, training, and partnerships
5. **Think beyond cost reduction** - pursue revenue and innovation use cases

### For CDOs:
1. **Evolve to data product thinking** - data as strategic asset, not just resource
2. **Prioritize real-time capabilities** - AI applications demand low-latency data
3. **Break down data silos** - federated and mesh architectures
4. **Invest in data quality** - foundation for AI success
5. **Partner with AI leadership** - collaborate closely with CAIO/CTO

### For Technology Leaders:
1. **Build scalable AI infrastructure** - cloud-native, elastic, cost-optimized
2. **Implement MLOps/LLMOps** - production-grade deployment and monitoring
3. **Design for agentic workflows** - enable agent-to-agent communication
4. **Prioritize observability** - understand AI system behavior
5. **Plan for model diversity** - multi-model, multi-provider strategies
